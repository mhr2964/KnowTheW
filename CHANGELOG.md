# Changelog — KnowTheW

## 2026-05-11
- **Era labels on the side of the team history table — click to scroll to and open that era's AI summary** — The Team History page now shows a vertical-text era label column to the left of the seasons table. Each label spans the rows for that era's year range (parsed from the era's record string) and is clickable — click to smooth-scroll the page to that era's summary card below and expand it. Era cards are now controlled (single-open) so clicking the same label twice collapses it, and clicking a different label moves the open state. Hidden on mobile (≤480px) since the side column doesn't fit; era cards still toggle directly there.
  Files: client/src/pages/TeamHistoryPage.jsx, client/src/App.css
- **Unblock Heroku releases by caching computed PBP results instead of raw game summaries** — The Mongo `gameSummaries` cache had grown to 515 MB (1394 docs at ~370 KB each, full raw ESPN summary blobs) and pushed the free tier over its 512 MB quota, causing the release-phase `seed-distributions.js` script's `bulkWrite` to fail and every recent deploy to be aborted at the release stage. Refactored the advanced-PBP path to cache the *computed* per-player-per-season result (`{ row, pbpGames }`, ~200 bytes) in a new `playerSeasonPbp` collection keyed by `playerId-season-seasontype`, matching the established pattern from `teamSeasonStats` / `teamSeasonInfo` / `teamHistories`. `fetchGameSummary` becomes a pure ESPN wrapper with no Mongo read or write. Cache write gates on a new `complete` flag so partial-fetch results (when ESPN flakes mid-batch) are never persisted. `buildPlayerIndex` and `seed-distributions.js` are now defensively wrapped — a cache-write failure logs and continues instead of crashing the release. A one-off `scripts/drop-legacy-game-summaries.js` (run via `heroku run` post-deploy) idempotently drops the now-unused `gameSummaries` collection to reclaim quota. Net effect: total PBP cache size drops by orders of magnitude (~500 MB → under 10 MB at saturation), and cache-warming scripts can no longer block a release.
  Files: server/lib/advancedStats.js, server/lib/espnClient.js, server/lib/percentileClient.js, scripts/seed-distributions.js, scripts/drop-legacy-game-summaries.js (new), DESIGN-pbp-cache-refactor.md (new)
- **Make team header and picker labels season-aware via franchise lineage** — When viewing a past season, the team header now reflects that season's historical franchise identity — name, record, seed, location — e.g., `/team/dallas-wings?season=2006` displays "Detroit Shock · 23-11 · 2nd in Eastern · Detroit" while keeping the Dallas Wings logo for continuity. A new `/api/teams/:id/season-info?season=YYYY` endpoint reuses historical standings logic and falls back to client-side lineage data if unavailable, ensuring the historical name always renders. Relocated teams (Dallas Wings, Las Vegas Aces, Connecticut Sun) now show contextualized year options in the picker dropdown — `2006 — Detroit Shock`, `2014 — Tulsa Shock`, `2001 — Orlando Miracle` — sourced from Wikipedia-confirmed franchise histories; unrelocated teams still show plain year options. Three new MongoDB collections (`teamSeasonStats`, `teamSeasonSchedule`, `teamSeasonInfo`) cache immutable past-season payloads indefinitely, with fire-and-forget writes and fallback to live ESPN fetch on miss. A new `confirmedEmpty` envelope flag distinguishes ESPN-verified empties (cacheable forever — e.g., teams that didn't make the playoffs) from transient errors (not cached, retry next request). `FRANCHISE_ALIASES` is now derived from `WNBA_FRANCHISE_LINEAGE` with module-load assertion, eliminating dual-source-of-truth drift for `isChampion` logic. `useLazyFetch` gained URL-change `setData(null)` to prevent cross-season header bleed during navigation. Known limitations (3 flagged by user-tester, non-blocking): invalid `?season=2030` leaves the bad param in URL despite fallback; no explicit loading indicator during 200-300ms header transitions; mobile 360px wraps long names like "San Antonio Silver Stars" to two lines. Note: MongoDB free tier is at quota (519/512 MB), so cache writes log warnings and degrade to direct espn fetches until quota is freed — pages work correctly, just not cached.

## 2026-05-10
- **Team History now ends with an AI-generated franchise narrative covering era overviews, key players, and team identity** — The narrative summarizes the team's franchise story across eras, names key players from training data with "(AI summary)" labels for transparency, and concludes with an identity paragraph. Narrative regenerates only when the championships list or season records change (content-addressed SHA-1 cache, no TTL), so costs are bounded to roughly once yearly per team.
